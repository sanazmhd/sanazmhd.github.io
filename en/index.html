📚Inside the Gas

Shlomo Venezia’s story is one of survival and unimaginable hardship. Born into a poor Jewish-Italian community, his early life was relatively safe under the protection of the Italians, but everything changed when the Germans invaded and deported his family to Auschwitz. Tragically, his mother and sisters were killed upon arrival, likely in the gas chambers. Faced with the harsh reality of survival, Venezia agreed to become a Sonderkommando, a role that involved performing some of the most grotesque and dehumanizing tasks imaginable: removing the bodies from the gas chambers and burning them in the crematoria....
